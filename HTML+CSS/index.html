Mercury

Mercury is the smallest and closest planet to the Sun in our solar system. It is a terrestrial planet with a heavily cratered surface, similar to the Moon. Mercury has no atmosphere and experiences extreme temperature variations, ranging from -180°C to 430°C.

  • Orbital Period: 88 days
  • Known Moons: 0
  • Interesting Fact: Mercury has the oldest known surface in the solar system, with some regions estimated to be over 4 billion years old.

Venus

Venus is the second planet from the Sun and is known for its thick, toxic atmosphere and scorching surface temperatures. It is the hottest planet in our solar system, with an average temperature of 462°C. Venus is also notable for its retrograde rotation, spinning slowly in the opposite direction of most planets.

  • Orbital Period: 225 days
  • Known Moons: 0
  • Interesting Fact: Venus is the only planet in our solar system to be named after a female figure from mythology.

Did You Know?

Jupiter's Great Red Spot is a storm larger than Earth, raging for over 400 years.

A day on Venus lasts longer than a year! Venus rotates so slowly that it takes 243 Earth days to complete one rotation.

Saturn is the least dense planet in our solar system. Its average density is less than that of water, meaning it could float in a bathtub if one were large enough!
